Emergence

Things are complicated.

It's tempting to try and re-derive the world from fundamentals, but this is a mistake.

Politics cannot be re-derived from speaking to individual people. Biology cannot be re-derived from studying individual atoms. In complex systems, the whole is greater than the sum of the parts.

And it's these extra features, these emergent properties, that create a never-ending supply of uncertainty.

The fundamentals are invaluable, but they are not everything.

How to account for this?

By incurring inefficiency. By leaving the world of perfect ideas, and entering reality. By tolerating the memorisation, and the arbitrary rules, and the imperfection that comes with complexity.

There is no substitute for a foundational understanding of the world, and it should not be skimped on. But to build something on top of this foundation, we need to get our hands dirty.

And once we do, the fun begins.
