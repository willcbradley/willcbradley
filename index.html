I love learning.

For as long as I can remember, I've been obsessed with acquiring new skills.
But during the end of high school, I noticed a concerning pattern.
And although I didn't know it at the time, this nagging frustration would soon shift my entire worldview.
Let me explain:
It started when I first decided to apply for medical school.
I did some research, and discovered that — with the right strategies — people could arbitrarily boost their applications.
Once I got into medical school, I saw how the most intelligent students could be outperformed by those who 'gamed the system'.
These systems were rewarding obedience, not competence.
Of course, this would be fine if reality played by our rules, but it doesn't.
I knew that, when the rubber met the road, these artificial accolades would be meaningless.
So out of frustration, I turned to the most unforgiving environment I could think of: the market.
I decided to try and build a robust skillset that could survive contact with reality (and complement a medical degree).
It's an endless process, and I'm documenting it here.
Feel free to explore using the links below. I hope you find it interesting.
