Thesis

Things are speeding up.

Today's world is nothing like our grandparents', and will be nothing like our grandchildren's.

In early medical school, this was disorienting.

It was hard to distinguish between the zero-sum career contest and the core ideas of medicine.

What was I supposed to stand for? Would new technology wipe out my job? What did it mean to be a good doctor?

People seemed to be avoiding these questions. And the biggest culprit, as far as I can see, was pessimism.

The assumption that one person's success is another's failure.

The insistence that ambition is fundamentally oppressive.

The claim that there are too many people on the planet.

These ideas were presented as truths, but they demoralised me. So I went back to basics, in search of a path forward.

From first principles, I tried to figure out what to do, and why, so that I didn't have to take anybody's word for it.

Now, it's time to put these ideas into practice.

I suspect it'll take a few decades before I'm competent enough to have much of an impact. Even then, I'll probably fail.

But the alternative is to give up.

To let pessimism win, and squander the world we've inherited.

That's no choice at all.

- Will


October 5th, 2025
