Thesis

Things are speeding up.

Today's world is nothing like our grandparents', and will be nothing like our grandchildren's.

In early medical school, this puzzled me. It was hard to distinguish between the zero-sum career contest and the fundamental essence of medicine.

What was I supposed to stand for? Would new technology wipe out my job? What did it mean to be a good doctor?

The culprit, as far as I can see, was pessimism.

The assumption that one person's success is another's failure.

The insistence that ambition is fundamentally oppressive.

The claim that there are too many people on the planet.

These ideas were presented as truths, but they demoralised me. So I went back to basics, in search of a path forward.

From first principles, I tried to figure out what to do, and why.

Now, it's time to put these principles into practice.

I expect it'll take a few decades before I'm competent enough to have much of an impact. Even then, I'll probably fail.

But the alternative is to give up.

To let pessimism win, and squander the world that's been left to us by our ancestors.

And that's no choice at all.

- Will
