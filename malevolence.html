Malevolence

Once, we were naive.

Failing to see the brutality of the world, we remained ignorant of our own circumstances.

But things changed.

Individually and collectively, our naivete was shattered.

We learned that the world was complex, that things were uncertain, that we could be hurt.

And in the process, we learned how to hurt others.

Nobody likes to think about malevolence, but it is undeniable.

We're all capable of terrible atrorcities (especially those who think they aren't).

How to act, in light of this?
