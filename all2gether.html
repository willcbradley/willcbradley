All Together

Life is extremely difficult.

To withstand this difficulty, it helps to have something fundamental to rely on (rather than shallow platitudes).

This requires a notion of "better" and "worse", forcing us to trade equality for reality.

To save us from the metabolically expensive act of solving everything from first principles, we can build structures to consolidate these value systems (both superficial and deep).

As the world becomes more leveraged, our choice of superficial structure(s) has a huge effect on our material outcomes.

But by placing the moral necessity of structure above material niceties of any given structure, we can avoid decision paralysis.

Maintaining these structures requires sacrifice, in line with their associated value structures.

This sacrifice only makes sense if we believe the future could possibly be better than the present.

As the pace of technological change accelerates, this belief becomes less an act of rationality, and more an act of courage.

And luckily, courage is always within our control.

What now?
