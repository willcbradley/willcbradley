Audacity

Pessimism is always beckoning.

It shelters us from disappointment, and makes us look wise, and frees us from responsibility.

It shuns forward-striving achievement, leaving us to shelter our egos in zero-sum, pseudo-egalitarian status games.

It makes today easier, at the expense of tomorrow.

But there's an alternative.

We can be audacious. We can set our sights so high that it justifies our suffering.

We can make the world better, according to our value systems.

And when corruption inevitably grows in the corpses of our great institutions, we can redeem them through courageous, precise, well-articulated dialogue.

There is no limit. No fixed prize that need to be divided up. We can create (and have created) something from nothing, enriching everybody in the process.

Humanity is constrained by itself. Not by the universe.

Maybe we'll fail. Maybe extinction is inevitable.

But we have to try.
